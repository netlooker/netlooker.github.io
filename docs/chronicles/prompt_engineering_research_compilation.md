# Research Compilation: Prompt Engineering and Context Engineering

This document provides a curated list of high-quality scientific papers focused on the fields of prompt engineering and context engineering, as part of the Netlooker research project.

## Research Papers

### 1. A Systematic Survey of Prompt Engineering in Large Language Models: Techniques and Applications
- **Authors:** Pranab Sahoo (Note: Full author list available in source)
- **Year:** 2024
- **Summary:** Provides a structured overview of recent advancements in prompt engineering, categorized by application area. It details prompting methodologies, applications, models, and datasets, offering a taxonomy of techniques.

### 2. The Prompt Report: A Systematic Survey of Prompt Engineering Techniques
- **Authors:** Sander Schulhoff, Michael Ilie, Nishant Balepur, Konstantine Kahadze, Amanda Liu, Chenglei Si, Yinheng Li, Aayush Gupta, HyoJung Han, Sevien Schulhoff, Pranav Sandeep Dulepet, Saurav Vidyadhara, Dayeon Ki, Sweta Agrawal, Chau Pham, Gerson Kroiz, Feileen Li, Hudson Tao, Ashay Srivastava, Hevander Da Costa, Saloni Gupta, Megan L. Rogers, Inna Goncearenco, Giuseppe Sarli, Igor Galynker, Denis Peskoff, Marine Carpuat, Jules White, Shyamal Anadkat, Alexander Hoyle, Philip Resnik
- **Year:** 2024
- **Summary:** A comprehensive survey that establishes a structured understanding of prompt engineering through a taxonomy of 58 LLM prompting techniques and 40 techniques for other modalities. It includes a detailed vocabulary and best practices for prompting SOTA LLMs.

### 3. Unleashing the potential of prompt engineering for large language models
- **Authors:** Banghao Chen, et al.
- **Year:** 2023 (Updated 2025)
- **Summary:** Explores foundational and advanced methodologies of prompt engineering, including self-consistency, chain-of-thought, and generated knowledge. It also examines prompt methods for Vision-Language Models (VLMs) like Context Optimization (CoOp) and Multimodal Prompt Learning (MaPLe).

### 4. Prompt engineering in ChatGPT for literature review: practical guide exemplified with studies on white phosphors
- **Authors:** Yookyoung Lee, Ju Hyun Oh, Dongik Lee, Mijeong Kang, Seunghun Lee
- **Year:** 2025
- **Summary:** Evaluates different prompt styles (Simple, Instruction, Markdown table, and Hybrid CoT) for information extraction from scientific literature, providing practical guidelines for scientists using LLMs.

### 5. Strengthening Human-Centric Chain-of-Thought Reasoning Integrity in LLMs via a Structured Prompt Framework
- **Authors:** Jiling Zhou (Note: Full author list available in source)
- **Year:** 2026
- **Summary:** Proposes a structured prompt engineering framework with 16 factors across four dimensions (Context/Scope, Evidence Grounding, Reasoning Structure, and Security Constraints) to improve reasoning integrity and security threat detection in local LLM deployments.

### 6. A Prompt Pattern Catalog to Enhance Prompt Engineering with ChatGPT
- **Authors:** Jules White (Note: Full author list available in source)
- **Year:** 2023
- **Summary:** Introduces a catalog of prompt engineering techniques presented in "pattern" form, providing reusable solutions to common problems in LLM interaction and output generation, analogous to software design patterns.

### 7. Prompt Engineering a Prompt Engineer
- **Authors:** Qinyuan Ye, et al.
- **Year:** 2023 (Accepted to ACL 2024)
- **Summary:** Introduces "PE2," a method that infuses meta-prompts with detailed descriptions, context specification, and step-by-step reasoning templates to automate and improve the quality of prompt engineering.

### 8. Chain-of-Thought Prompting Elicits Reasoning in Large Language Models
- **Authors:** Jason Wei, et al.
- **Year:** 2022
- **Summary:** A seminal paper demonstrating that providing a few chain-of-thought demonstrations as exemplars significantly improves the ability of large language models to perform complex arithmetic, commonsense, and symbolic reasoning tasks.
